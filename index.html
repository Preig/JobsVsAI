Sources: Felten, Raj, Seamans (2021) AIOE Language Modeling exposure index | O*NET 29.2 ability importance | BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook (May 2024 employment) | BLS CPS 2024 Annual Averages Table 11 (sex composition).
Methodology in plain language. The horizontal axis is each occupation's Language Modeling AIOE from Felten et al. (2021), rescaled to 0–1. Higher means more of the occupation's tasks overlap with what large language models can already do. The vertical axis is an adaptability composite built from O*NET ability importance scores: 70% the maximum of an occupation's score on either creative-cognitive abilities (originality, fluency of ideas, problem sensitivity, inductive reasoning, visualization, category flexibility) or physical-anchor abilities (manual dexterity, multilimb coordination, stamina, etc.); 30% creative-only score; with a 40% override blending in BLS entry-education and median pay. The intuition: jobs whose value comes from abilities AI is bad at, or that require advanced human judgment, are more durable. Vulnerability = exposure minus adaptability. HIGH if > 0.10, LOW if < -0.10, MEDIUM in between. 259 occupations included; figures reflect publicly available data as of May 2026. Confidence in relative ranking: high (~85%); confidence in exact numerical positions: moderate (~65%) because the adaptability composite is a constructed proxy, not a published index.
